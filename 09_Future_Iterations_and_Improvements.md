# Future Iterations and Improvements

### Evolving the Playbook Over Time

## Common Pitfalls and Anti‑Patterns

- Treating fairness as a one‑time check instead of an ongoing process.
- Relying only on post‑processing without addressing data or model issues.
- Failing to involve domain experts or HR/DEI in fairness decisions.
- Not documenting trade‑offs, making decisions hard to defend later.
- Monitoring only accuracy while ignoring fairness drift.

## Known Limitations

- Manual governance decisions.
- Overhead for small teams.
- Limited automation in fairness monitoring.

## Planned Improvements

### Tooling Integration

### Organizational Maturity

## Feedback Mechanisms

- Quarterly feedback from teams.
- Annual leadership review.
- Continuous improvement cycle.

## Fairness Maturity Model

- **Level 1 – Awareness:**  
  Ad‑hoc fairness discussions, no formal processes.

- **Level 2 – Process Adoption:**  
  Basic fairness checks in some projects, limited documentation.

- **Level 3 – Integrated Governance:**  
  Governance structures, playbook adoption, and regular reviews in place.

- **Level 4 – Automated Monitoring:**  
  Fairness metrics integrated into CI/CD and monitoring systems.

- **Level 5 – Continuous Optimization:**  
  Fairness objectives regularly revisited, data and models iteratively improved.
